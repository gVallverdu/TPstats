Short introduction to statistical representations

This page gives a short introduction to statistics and statistical representations used in this web application. The histogram plot, the box plot and the normal distribution are briefly presented. We aim at giving a few notion in order for you to be able to understand the plots provided by the application. You are strongly encouraged to look at the reference material at the bottom of this page.

You said histogram ?

A histogram is a graphical representation of the distribution of a continuous variable. In order to plot an histogram from a set of N measures you have to run over the following steps :

  1. Chose a range in order to plot your histogram.
  2. Divide the interval into bins.
  3. For each measure, identify in which bin they stand.
  4. Count the number of measure in each bin.
  5. Plot the number of measures in a bin as ordinates and the center of the bins as abscissa.

In this example, we asked students to measure a volume of 10 mL with a beaker and plot the histogram of their measurements. The range is between 6 and 12 mL with 12 bins. Box plots

The opposite plot is called a boxplot and is usefull to take a summarised view of the distribution of a data sample through their quartiles. All the data are depicted on the left of the box plot, as circles.

The quartiles divide the data in four equal groups with each group containing a quarter of the data. After you have sorted your data from the lowest to the highest the quartiles are defined as follow :

Median, or Q2
cut the data in two equal parts and is defined as the number for which 50% of the data are lower and 50% are higher.
Q1
25% of the data belong between Q1 and the smallest value.
Q3
25% of the data belong between Q3 and the highest value.

As a consequence, 50% of the data belong between Q1 and Q3. This interval is also called IQR for Interquartile range.

One adavantage of the quartiles and the boxplot is that they give a summarized view of the distribution and allow to look at the dissymmetry of the distribution depending on the position of the quartiles.

The normal distribution

The normal or gaussian distribution is hugely used in order to describe the distribution of some data. The mathematical expression of the distribution reads: $$g(x, \mu, \sigma) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} \, \exp\left( -\frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right)$$ where \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\) are called the moments of the distribution and stand for the average and the standard deviation, respectively.

The data used here are the same as the one used for the previous histogram concerning the measures of the volume with a beaker. The moments of the normal distributions are:

Considering a normal distribution, 68.27% of the data belong to the intervall that is equal to \(2\sigma\) and center around the mean \(\mu\).

Using a normal distribution is convenient because its expression is simple and one can find mathematical tools to work with normal distribution.

A skewed distribution

Skewed distribution are used in order to take into account the skewness of a distribution. Here we used a normal distribution multiplied by its cummulative density function. The whole skewed distribution reads

$$s(x, \mu, \sigma, \alpha) = A \exp\left( -\frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) \left[1 + \text{erf}\left(\alpha \frac{x-\mu}{\sigma\sqrt{2}}\right)\right]$$

where erf is a function, called the error function corresponding to the integral of a normal distribution between 0 and x. The parameter \(\alpha\) can be viewed as the skeweness of the distribution. It could be positive or negative.

The data used here are the same as the one used for the previous histogram concerning the measures of the volume with a beaker. The parameters of the skewed distribution are :

\(\mu\)\(\sigma\)\(\alpha\)
10.371.24-1.73

The parameters \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\) are slightly different than those of the normal distribution. Here a negative skeweness of \(\alpha\) = -1.73 means that the distribution falls more slowly for the smaller values than for the higher ones.
